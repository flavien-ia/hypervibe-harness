# /prof

Explains to you in plain language how Hypervibe works and what each technical building block is for. With everyday analogies and as many questions as you want, so you can become self-sufficient without having to be a developer.

## When to use it

- You're discovering the plugin and you want to understand how it works **before** using it
- You want to know what a given command is for (`/add-stripe`, `/add-i18n`...) before running it
- You have a question about the technical stack (what is Next.js? What is Vercel for?)
- You want to understand what "vibe coding", "deployment", or "the specification" means

## How it works

Hypervibe offers you a **table of contents with 8 topics**:

1. **The principle**: How it works, in 2 minutes
2. **The technical stack**: The building blocks of your app (and what each one is for)
3. **The bootstrap**: What happens when you run `/bootstrap`
4. **Everyday tools**: The commands you'll use regularly
5. **Add-ons**: The features we add depending on your needs
6. **Deployment**: How your app goes from your computer to the internet
7. **The specification**: How to properly describe what you want to build
8. **Vibe coding**: How to evolve your app after the bootstrap

You choose the number that interests you, or you ask your own question in free language. Hypervibe replies with analogies (a restaurant, a building, the mail, etc.), explains each technical term the moment it comes up, and invites you to ask more questions at the end.

If you say "everything" or "start from the beginning", it goes through the 8 topics in order.

## What it creates for you

Nothing on your computer, it's a purely conversational session. But by the end, you understand:
- What a "web app" is and how it's built
- What GitHub, Vercel, Neon, and the other services do
- Why Hypervibe makes this or that technical choice
- How to go about evolving your app

## Prerequisites

None. `/prof` needs nothing, you can run it even without an existing project, just to understand.

## Tips

{{callout:tip|First time using a new skill}}
When you discover a new plugin skill (`/add-stripe`, `/seo`, `/clean`...), run `/prof` beforehand to ask: "Explain to me what `/add-stripe` does". You'll then run the skill knowing what to expect.
{{/callout}}

{{callout:info|You can also ask open-ended questions}}
The table of contents is just a starting point. If you want to understand something that isn't in it (for example "what is an API?" or "why is GDPR important?"), just ask the question directly.
{{/callout}}
